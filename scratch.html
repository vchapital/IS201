History

Volleyball, conceived by William G. Morgan in 1895, originated in Holyoke, Massachusetts, as an indoor sport designed to be a less strenuous alternative to basketball. Originally called "mintonette," the game evolved quickly, with its name changed to volleyball due to the nature of the volleying action involved. By the early 20th century, volleyball had gained popularity, spreading to various YMCA centers and schools across the United States. The sport's international growth surged after World War I, with the formation of the Fédération Internationale de Volleyball (FIVB) in 1947, overseeing its global development. The introduction of beach volleyball in California during the 1920s added a new dimension to the sport's appeal. Over the decades, volleyball continued to evolve in rules, techniques, and strategies, becoming one of the most widely played sports worldwide, featured prominently in Olympic competitions and cherished for its fast-paced, dynamic nature.

Rules

  1. Point System
    • A point is awarded at the end of every play.
    • The team who won the point gets to 'serve', or begin the play by hitting the ball from their side to the opponent's side.
    • When the ball hits the floor, a player commits an illegal move, or at the referee's whistle, the play is over.
  2. The Court
    • If the ball lands inside of the court enclosed by lines, it it considered 'in'.
    • If the ball touches any part of the lines, it is considered 'in'.
    • Players are allowed to move freely in and out of the court, including outside of the lines, except for the line is shared by the opposing team, the center line.
  3. The Ball
    • Players can touch any part of the ball with any part of their body.
    • Touching the ball with your forearms, via passing, and with your hands, via setting and hitting, is most efficient.
    • If the ball lands 'out' or outside of the lines of the court, the team who did NOT touch it last is awarded the point.
  4. The Net
    • Players are never permitted to touch the net. If they do, the point is awarded to the opposig team automatically.
    • The ball is allowed to touch the net any any time, and for an inifinte number of times in single play or game.
  5. Players
    • A player can interact with their own teammates at no penalty.
    • Opposing players are not permitting to interact or touch at any time during a play.
    • Players can be rotated through all 6 different positions on the court. The only except are liberos, a special passer that follows a different set of rules.
